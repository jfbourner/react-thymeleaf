Raspberry PI cluster

Welcome to the backend! But no, seriously, this page explains how this website and the steps I went through to get it to the place it is today. Enjoy :)

My mission

Having worked in a large bank for a number of years I was getting a bit fed up not having the resources I needed to hone my skills and try out the latest technologies. Using the command line, docker, kubernetes etc. on the daily but not being able to "poke" around left me frustrated. I want to be able to break things without being court marshalled into a meeting. Then, one evening while browsing YouTube, I stumbled across Jeff Geering, who had put together his own cluster of raspberry PI's. This got my thinking of how I could do the same and run my own kubernetes cluster, from where I could build, deploy and break (Not too much)

Setting up the cluster

As one of my most exciting birthday presents to date, 4 raspberry PI4's were gifted to myself. It came with 4 SD cards and 4 chargers, but it soon because apparent that I needed a cleaner setup. I can't be dealing with all those wires and I haven't even got to the ethernet cables yet. With this I purchased a power bank, to power all 4 Pis, as well as an ethernet switch with 4, short, ethernet cables.

Time to flash the SD cards and install Pi OS(Formally know as Raspbian). I used the official raspberry pi imagerfound here At the time, only the 32 bit OS had a stable release and so I foolishly went with it.
